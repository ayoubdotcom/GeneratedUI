Ancient Architecture

Characterized by massive structures with thick walls, columns, and minimal openings. The pyramids, ziggurats, and temples emphasized symmetry and geometric precision.

3000 BC - 500 BC

Classical

Greek and Roman architecture introduced the classical orders (Doric, Ionic, Corinthian) with emphasis on proportion, symmetry, and mathematical precision. The arch and dome became significant innovations.

500 BC - 500 AD

Gothic

Known for pointed arches, ribbed vaults, and flying buttresses that allowed taller buildings with more glass. Cathedrals like Notre-Dame emphasized verticality and light.

1100 - 1500 AD
